HB 381

Electric Mobility Device Amendments

Passed · 2026

Rep. Paul Cutler · Sen. Todd Weiler

Establishes a helmet requirement for e-bike riders under 21, and beginning May 2027, requires riders 8–15 to complete an online safety course to ride without adult supervision. Passed House 49–19, Senate 18–10.

Read the bill →
My Role

Drafted the bill with House Transportation Committee staff. Worked with Rep. Cutler and stakeholders to negotiate provisions and adjust the bill as it moved through committee.

↻
HB 290

Bicycle Lane Safety Amendments

Passed · 2025

Rep. Verona Mauga · Sen. Todd Weiler

Prohibits driving or parking in bike lanes statewide, with limited exceptions, and clarifies city/UDOT responsibilities when a bike lane must be temporarily obstructed. Effective May 7, 2025.

Read the bill →
My Role

Drafted this bill with Rep. Mauga and worked to convince legislators to support it. Hosted a lobbying day event featuring Rep. Mauga, where constituents could talk with her directly about the bill.

↻
HB 449

Pedestrian Safety and Facilities Act Modifications

Passed · 2024

Rep. Nelson Abbott · Sen. Kathleen Riebe

Amends the state's Pedestrian Safety and Facilities Act to explicitly include cyclists, requiring cities and UDOT to consider cyclist safety in planning and engineering decisions.

Read the bill →
My Role

Drafted the legislation with Rep. Abbott. Worked with him to negotiate provisions with UDOT and WFRC to get them on board in support of the bill.

HB 554

Bicycle Rack Amendments

Did not pass · 2025

Rep. Verona Mauga

Would have created a Bike Rack Matching Grant Pilot Program in the Governor's Office, providing $50k in annual matching funds for municipalities to install and maintain public bike racks. Did not receive a hearing.

Read the draft →
My Role

Drafted the bill and proposed the matching-grant model on behalf of Bike Utah. It didn't receive a committee hearing.

Did not pass · 2023

Would have eliminated minimum parking requirements for developments within a half-mile of FrontRunner, TRAX, or BRT stations. Did not pass, but influenced ongoing housing and zoning reform discussions.
